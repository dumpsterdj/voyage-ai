INTRO:
There is a moment, somewhere around the 4th hairpin bend above Kaza, when the valley opens up below you like something that shouldn't exist. A river silver as a mirror threading through a lunar landscape. Monasteries the colour of the rock they sit on. Sky so blue it looks painted.

Spiti doesn't feel like India. It doesn't feel like anywhere you've been. That's exactly why you should go.
---
SECTION: Getting There — The Two Routes
**Via Manali (Rohtang Pass):** Open June–October only. Rohtang closes with the first heavy snow. Manali to Kaza takes 10–12 hours on roads that vary from rough to barely-there. The scenery makes every kilometre worth it.

**Via Shimla (Kinnaur):** Open longer, possible year-round in good winters. Shimla to Kaza takes 12–14 hours but the Kinnaur valley section along the Sutlej gorge is jaw-dropping.

Best approach: **enter via Manali, exit via Shimla** (or reverse). Doing a loop gives you both valleys and avoids retracing the same road.
---
SECTION: Kaza — Your Base Camp
Most travellers base themselves in **Kaza** (3,800m). It's the largest town in Spiti, which means it has a handful of guesthouses, two ATMs (bring cash — they often run out), and the world's highest post office at nearby Hikkim.

Stay at **Zostel Spiti** or **Sakya Abode** — both have reliable hot water and serve dal-rice that tastes better than anywhere at sea level because you've been hiking at altitude all day.

Acclimatise for a full day before doing anything strenuous.
---
SECTION: Key Monasteries
- **Key Monastery** — 4km from Kaza, perched on a 4,166m hill. The oldest in Spiti (11th century). Arrive at 6 AM for morning prayers — the monks chanting in the low light is one of those sounds you carry home.
- **Tabo Monastery** — the "Ajanta of the Himalayas". The mud-walled murals inside are 1,000 years old and astonishingly preserved. No photography inside.
- **Dhankar Monastery** — clinging to a cliff edge 300m above the confluence of the Spiti and Pin rivers. The hike up takes 20 minutes and the view from the top is unreasonable.
---
SECTION: Chandratal Lake
4,300m. A glacial lake so blue it looks photoshopped. It's a 14km drive from Batal followed by a 3km walk on a rocky trail. Go in the morning — clouds roll in by afternoon and obscure the reflection.

Camping near the lake is permitted (and magical). Temperatures drop to -5°C even in July. Carry a sleeping bag rated to -10°C minimum.
---
CLOSING:
I left Spiti lighter somehow — less attached to the ordinary comforts that feel essential in cities. The altitude forces a slowness that cities never allow. You walk slower, breathe more deliberately, eat simpler food and sleep harder than you have in years.

The landscapes are extraordinary. But the thing that stays with you is the quiet. In Spiti, silence isn't the absence of sound — it's something present and full.

Go slowly. Stay longer than you planned.
